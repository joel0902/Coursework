Communication can cause a project to fail. Using a programme such as Trello, Slack or an email would help to prevent failure
Time - Time is needed to make sure the project is completed within the allotted time
Change of requirement - A competitive market can lead to a quick change of vision and because of this clients may sometimes change their vision of how their product or certain features in it should look like.
Unforeseen circumstances - If someone was to get sick for example it may delay the project for a certain amount of time or it could even derail it.
Neglecting design could save time and money but since design plays a crucial aspect about developement this could lead projects to fail
Technical risks should as systems failing or budget cuts could force you into a situation where you need to satisfy a clients requirements while being low on resources
Unavoidable risks such as government policy changes or technologies being discontinued


1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software - how do you select the right kind of technology for the job.

Choosing what you want the software to do is the first point. You need to know what you want the system to do, what problems it should solve, and what the solution would be worth. Creating a list of software requirements can help you to become a little more specific e.g ease of use, technological preferences, budget requirements, reporting requirements, vendor requirement. Eventually narrowing them down into "fundamental" and "nice to have" A software comparison site could help you to compare what they offer and how it fits into your needs. You need to think about your companies vision, which will involve trialling and testing demos of software and systems. Once you have conducted some research, identify a list of potential software and systems. Ask for a demonstration of how some company's solution addresses all your requirements. Narrow down your list by focusing on products that have impressed you.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects - how does GDPR impact digital projects

GDPR impacts digital projects such as website creation as the regulations are stricter in regards to how personal details are collected and used like sensitive information that can be use to identify someone, This applys to anyones personal or sensitive data and how it is used can be a breach of GDPR regulations, explicit permission must be gained from the owner of the information. You can't just use someones personal information for your website or collect online identifiers (i.e someones device MAC address, IP or User account ID's) A user is entitled to "privacy by demand" which means if they give consent (opt-in) you can user their data for the goal they gave permission on and they will always be able to withdraw their consent meaning ALL data collected from this user from the data must be removed.
